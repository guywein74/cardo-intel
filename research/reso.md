# RESO / ResoSport — Competitive Intelligence Notes (for Cardo Systems)

Researched: 2026-07-02. Companion data file: `/Users/guyw/Desktop/Claude/Cardo/research/reso.json`

## 1. Company overview

- **Who**: Reso (resosport.com, LinkedIn: "RESO" / reso-intercom) makes motorcycle helmet mesh communicators. Founding story: a team of **former DJI engineers** (algorithms, signal processing, system design) inspired by a communication failure on a fall 2021 mountain ride; ~3 years of development; brand launched **late 2024**.
- **HQ**: Shenzhen, China per Tracxn (founded 2024). The company website deliberately does not disclose HQ, registration, or funding. No funding rounds found in public sources.
- **Leadership**: Zigwheels PH quotes a "CEO Julius King" at the March 2025 PH launch — unclear whether this is the global CEO or the Philippine distributor's CEO. Treat with caution.
- **Positioning**: "Communication gear built for those who refuse to be out of range." Premium flagship features (mesh, GPS/SOS, AI noise reduction, camera integration) at roughly 60-80% of Cardo/Sena flagship pricing, wrapped in a DJI-pedigree engineering narrative.
- **Go-to-market**: Launched first in **Southeast Asia (Philippines)** rather than the West — public debut at Makina Moto Expo, Manila, April 4-6, 2025. Now: US/global DTC Shopify store (USD, 2-day shipping, 30-day trial, free returns), PH dealer network (Motojini, Ridebuy, Alpha Lifestyle Club, Takong Racing, Bandidos Pitstop), Malaysia (MRoute19), UAE (Motofever), eBay. **No Amazon, RevZilla, or major Western brick-and-mortar presence found** as of July 2026.

## 2. Product line (as of July 2026)

Three-tier lineup: **Pilot Lite → Pilot Neo → Pilot Pro**, plus accessories.

### Reso Pilot Pro (flagship, late 2024)
- **Price**: $399 MSRP / $359 site sale. Motojini: $250 ($288 reg). Philippines: PHP 12,500 launch, PHP 13,999 in Oct 2025 review (~USD 220-245). Note the large US-vs-SEA price gap.
- **Intercom**: "DuoSync" = hardware mesh + **software mesh over the phone's hotspot/cellular** with automatic switching, simultaneous with Bluetooth 5.2. This IP-relay fallback is their signature differentiator vs Cardo/Sena.
- **Range/riders**: 2.5 km point-to-point, 12 km group relay, up to 30 riders; "unlimited" via software mesh (requires mobile data).
- **Audio**: self-developed 40 mm speakers; optional **45 mm "Audio X" HD speaker** upgrade ($99/$69). Self-developed AI noise reduction claimed to 110 mph / 180 kph. No brand audio partner (no JBL/Harman equivalent) — they explicitly pitch their in-house speakers as "on par with Cardo's JBL."
- **Battery**: 1950 mAh; 13 h mesh+music, 18 h music-only, 33 h power-saving; 15W fast charge (80% in 30 min, full 50-60 min, 5 min = ~2 h).
- **Waterproof**: IP67.
- **Safety**: integrated **GNSS + gyroscope; automatic SOS/crash alert** to designated emergency contacts.
- **Other**: "Hey RESO" wake word with continuous-listening command mode; gyro double-tap to answer calls; Reso Link app (route book, live teammate GPS tracking, remote grouping, OTA); music sharing; intercom recording; USB/BT action-cam integration (GoPro, DJI, Insta360) that **mixes team intercom audio into camera footage** — strong moto-vlogger hook.
- **Warranty**: 36 months (accessories 12 months). In box: unit, magnetic base + adhesive mount, clip mount, 40 mm speakers, wired + boom mics, USB-C cable, velcro.

### Reso Pilot Neo (mid, 2025)
- **Price**: $320 MSRP / $269 sale; $179 seen in one regional listing; UAE AED 800 (~$218).
- Single-channel hardware mesh + BT 5.2 (no software mesh). 2.5 km / 12 km group, **15 riders**. Same 1950 mAh battery, quoted **up to 24 h talk**; 15W fast charge; IP67; 150 ms latency; AI noise reduction; voice control; app + OTA; music sharing; camera integration; intercom recording. **No GNSS, no SOS, no real-time map.** 3-yr warranty.
- Spec conflict: one dealer calls it "world's first dual-channel mesh"; the official site describes a single mesh channel. Official spec used.

### Reso Pilot Lite (entry, ~late 2025)
- Bluetooth-only intercom (per dealer listing title). Only confirmed price: **RM399 (~USD 85-90)** at MRoute19 Malaysia. Not on the US store; specs not publicly documented. Appears aimed at SEA budget segment.

### Reso Headset ($149/$119)
- App-connected headset (likely half-helmet/commuter use); currently **sold out** on resosport.com; almost no published specs. PH launch price PHP 4,800.

### Accessories
- 45 mm HD Speaker: $99/$69. Second Helmet Kit (Pro/Neo): $129/$109. Extra cradle in PH: PHP 2,500.

## 3. Positioning vs Cardo and Sena

- Press and dealers explicitly frame Pilot Pro against **Cardo Packtalk Pro and Sena 50S/60S** at a much lower price.
- Their implicit anti-Cardo pitch: (a) hybrid mesh with unlimited IP range vs DMC's RF-only limits; (b) built-in GNSS SOS; (c) hands-free "Hey RESO" continuous listening they claim beats "Hey Cardo"; (d) in-house speakers "as good as JBL" for less money; (e) camera-audio recording for vloggers.
- Reviewer consensus on the matchup (MotoMelody, phtoll, TopGear PH): **Cardo still wins music/audio fidelity (JBL)**; **Sena wins mixed-brand group interoperability (Open Mesh)**; **Reso wins voice-first UX, value, and battery/charging**, best for solo riders or same-brand small groups.

## 4. Review sentiment (strengths / weaknesses detail)

Positive (TopGear PH long-term, phtoll, Ridebuy PH, YouTube "This Helmet Intercom WILL REPLACE Cardo"):
- Noise reduction "shockingly good"; crystal-clear voice over loud exhausts; premium build; precise magnetic cradle; intuitive one-button power + knob controls; voice commands "work the first time"; 13-19 h real-world battery; 5-min top-up = 2 h; TopGear PH judged speakers subjectively better than Packtalk Bold's JBL units even after months of use.

Negative / risks:
- **Network-effect problem**: tiny installed base, no cross-brand mesh — the #1 con in every review.
- Software mesh needs cellular coverage — fails exactly where extended range is most needed (remote ADV riding).
- Accidental voice-command triggers during normal conversation reported.
- Switching costs (proprietary cradles) deter Cardo/Sena converts.
- Almost all credible reviews originate from the Philippines/SEA; near-zero organic Reddit/US forum footprint — Western awareness is minimal.
- No disclosed funding, unclear corporate structure, service network thin outside SEA.

## 6. Implications for Cardo (analyst take)

1. Reso is currently a **regional (SEA) value threat**, not yet a Western channel threat — but the US DTC store, USD pricing, and 3-yr warranty signal Western ambitions.
2. Their **IP-based "unlimited range" software mesh** and **camera-audio recording** are marketable differentiators Cardo should evaluate/neutralize in messaging (cellular dependency is the counterargument).
3. Watch for: Amazon/RevZilla listing, a distributor announcement in EU/US, funding news, or a Pilot Pro price cut — any would escalate the threat level.
4. The 30-40% SEA-vs-US price differential suggests margin room to discount aggressively if they push West.

## Dimensions data (added 2026-07-02, Cardo landscape format)

Per-product "dimensions" blocks added to reso.json. Sourcing notes and ambiguities:

### Confirmed hard specs
- **Pilot Pro and Pilot Neo share the same chassis**: 88.7 x 49.6 x 22.9 mm, **65 g without base** (dealer spec listings, e.g., Bandidos Pitstop / Takong Racing; consistent across sources). Slim profile. The shared "Pilot Pro/Neo 2nd Helmet Kit" on resosport.com confirms a common magnetic mount platform.
- Controls (Pro, from TopGear PH long-term review): volume knob + 3 buttons, single-button power, gyro double-tap to answer calls. Mounts included: magnetic base + adhesive mount + clip mount. No screw-clamp mount documented.
- Latency: 150 ms claimed for both Pro and Neo.

### Ambiguities / judgment calls (flagged so the dashboard doesn't overstate certainty)
1. **Wideband audio**: not mentioned in any Reso spec sheet, manual listing, or review — set to null for all products. Their marketing says "High-Fidelity Sound" but never specifies codec/bandwidth.
2. **HD flag**: Pilot Pro set hd=true on the strength of "High-Fidelity Sound" marketing and the optional 45mm "Audio X HD" upgrade; Neo left null (same drivers claimed but no HD language found for Neo specifically).
3. **Auto-reconnect**: Pro set true — DuoSync's documented automatic switching between hardware and software mesh is an auto-reconnect mechanism, and reviews describe seamless reconnection. Neo left null: standard mesh rejoin is likely but not explicitly documented.
4. **Neo controls/voice**: assumed same control platform as Pro given identical chassis; gyro tap gestures and wake-word behavior not independently verified for Neo — worded as "assumed" in JSON.
5. **Cross-brand pairing**: no source documents a universal Bluetooth intercom pairing mode with Cardo/Sena (unlike most competitors, this is conspicuously absent from marketing). Reviewers (phtoll, MotoMelody) describe cross-brand compatibility as "limited" and recommend same-brand groups. Treated as "not documented" rather than "absent" — worth verifying with a physical unit.
6. **Pilot Lite and Reso Headset**: no weight, dimensions, controls, or audio specs published anywhere found; dimension blocks are mostly null. The manuals.plus Reso Pilot manual (403-blocked) may contain more — retry later or pull the PDF manual from resosport.com/pages/user-manual with a browser.
